About Yitong

Be honest, work very hard, laugh a lot.

I’m a designer interested in helping people work better. In college, I studied how people work together in groups. During that time, I also started a startup that helps companies get their paperwork done faster.

Recently, my interest has shifted from group work to creative work. I’m fascinated by the creative process of designers, movie makers, and comic artists. To put my passion to practice, I’m building a tool that makes working with typography easier for beginners.

College — 50% founder, 30% student, 20% cockroach

In college, I tried to make good on my highschool dreams of starting a company. I worked on school by day and hacked away on my startup by night.

In the moment, we felt like cockroaches: sleeping in an unheated basement, working long hours, eating instant noodles. But looking back, I’m grateful for the experience because it taught me to work hard and to use limited resources to solve problems.

High school — maybe an artist, maybe a capitalist

Like most immigrant children, I was always expected to be a doctor or a lawyer. In my teenage rebellion, I decided instead to throw myself into the world of literature, philosophy and art. I read everything from And Rand to Albert Camus, made art, and discussed politics with the theatre kids.

Being an impressionable teen, I decided to follow the footsteps of my literary heroes by becoming a capitalist and an artist. The latter never happened (though not for lack of trying), but I did end up going to business school.

About Yitong

Getting a cab in NYC is hard. Teamwork is hard. In fact, life in general is pretty hard. As a product designer, I embrace that because I believe in design’s potential to untangle the hardest problems around us. Good design carries us over the hurdles and enables us to live better, fuller lives — with our friends, at work, and beyond.

While my passion lies in UI and UX, I love messing with code to bring my ideas to life. And as a former startup founder, I’m comfortable relentlessly iterating from UI → UX → code in search of a better solution. See more of my story →
