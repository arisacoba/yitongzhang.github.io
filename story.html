Yitong Zhang

Be honest, work very hard, don’t take life too seriously

I’m a designer interested in helping people work better. For my undergrad, I studied how people work together in groups. During that time, I also started a startup that helps companies get their paperwork done faster.

These days, my interest has shifted from group work to creative work. I’m fascinated by the creative process of designers, movie makers, and comic artists. To put my passion to practice, I’m building a tool that makes working with typography easier for beginners.

More About Yitong

College — 50% founder, 30% student, 20% cockroach

In college, I tried to get my startup off the ground while taking a full course load.

In the moment, we felt like cockroaches: sleeping in an unheated basement, working long hours, eating instant noodles. Looking back however, I’m grateful for the experience because the work ethic and efficiency we developed during that time far outweighed the discomfort.

High school — maybe an artist, maybe a capitalist

Like most immigrant children, I was always expected to be a doctor or a lawyer. In my teenage rebellion, I decided instead to throw myself into the world of literature, philosophy and art. I read everything from Friedrich Hayek to Albert Camus, made art, and discussed politics with theatre kids.

Somewhere along the way, I decided that I was either going to become a capitalist or an artist and because my parents were never going to let me go to art school, I went to business school for college.

About Yitong

Getting a cab in NYC is hard. Teamwork is hard. In fact, life in general is pretty hard. As a product designer, I embrace that because I believe in design’s potential to untangle the hardest problems around us. Good design carries us over the hurdles and enables us to live better, fuller lives — with our friends, at work, and beyond.

While my passion lies in UI and UX, I love messing with code to bring my ideas to life. And as a former startup founder, I’m comfortable relentlessly iterating from UI → UX → code in search of a better solution. See more of my story →
